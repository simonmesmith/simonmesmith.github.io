* In 2000, Goldman Sachs employed 600 traders at its New York headquarters. Today, there are just two left, with automated trading programs taking over the bulk of the work.[^1]
* BlackRock, the world's biggest money manager, cut more than 40 portfolio management jobs in 2017 and replaced them with AI stock traders.[^2]
* Japan's third biggest lender, Mizuho Financial Group Inc., has announced that it, too, will use AI for equity trading for institutional clients.[^3] (It strikes me that, if AI proves itself a more sane, stable and successful investor, institutional clients and their stakeholders will _demand_ it, further driving automation of the financial sector.)
* AI investing isn't limited to public markets. Venture capital firms are also using machine learning to increase their odds of successful investment.[^4]
* [Numerai][5], an hedge fund that uses AI, crowdsourcing and cryptocurrencies, had 30,000 data scientists using the platform as of October 2017, which is 100 times more than any other hedge fund in the world after just two years in existence.[^5]
* In October 2017, a new academic paper, provided conclusive evidence that AI can independently discover profitable trading strategies.[^6]
