* Law is one area where AI is making huge inroads, including in e-discovery as well as decision-support for such activities as parole review. McKinsey estimates that 23% of a lawyer’s job can be automated.[^1]
* [ROSS Intelligence][2], which uses AI to expedite legal research, is proving this. Their technology can reportedly reduce a week’s worth of legal research to between 2 and 4 hours.[^2]
* While tools like ROSS are built _for_ lawyers, there are direct-to-consumer examples of AI replacing lawyers entirely. For example, [DoNotPay][4] is a chatbot that fights parking tickets and even helped people sue Equifax for its data breach.[^3]
