* In September 2017, researchers announced creating a fully automated farm in which autonomous robots planted, tended and harvested a crop of barley.[^1]

* [Modular Science](http://www.modularscience.com/) is building “modular machines for agriculture” (though its website was quite sparse as of September 2017).

* [Abundant Robotics](https://www.abundantrobotics.com/) promises “robotic solutions for the hardest jobs in agriculture.” One of these solutions is an apple picking robot that sucks fruit off trees[^2]—an important development given the difficulty of harvesting fruit with robots without damaging it.

* Agricultural automation isn’t limited to small, disruptive outsiders. John Deere, for example, is investing heavily in automation technology. It spent $305 million to buy robotics company Blue River Technology, which makes agricultural robots able to deliver herbicides more precisely where needed.[^3]
