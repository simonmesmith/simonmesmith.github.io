* Retail jobs are likely to face near-term automation (while simultaneously being made unnecessary by the growth of ecommerce). Some predictions call for 80% of retail jobs to disappear within the coming decades.[^1] A 2017 report from investment advisory firm Cornerstone Capital Group estimated that 6 to 7.5 million retail jobs will soon be rendered obsolete by automation.[^2]
* Nearly two-thirds of Americans think that most retailers will be fully automated within 20 years.[^3]
* One company enabling staff-less stores is Standard Cognition, which uses a network of cameras along with machine vision and deep learning to automate checkouts and eliminate cashiers.[^4]
* Convenience stores might be the first to go full automation. For example, a Chinese startup called Xingbianli raised USD$15.2 million via angel financing to open staff-less convenience stores.[^5]
