* Retail jobs are likely to face near-term automation (while simultaneously being made unnecessary by the growth of ecommerce). Some predictions call for 80% of retail jobs to disappear within the coming decades.[^1]

* Nearly two-thirds of Americans think that most retailers will be fully automated within 20 years.[^2] 

* One company enabling staff-less stores is Standard Cognition, which uses a network of cameras along with machine vision and deep learning to automate checkouts and eliminate cashiers.[^3]

* Convenience stores might be the first to go full automation. For example, a Chinese startup called Xingbianli raised USD$15.2 million via angel financing to open staff-less convenience stores.[^4]
