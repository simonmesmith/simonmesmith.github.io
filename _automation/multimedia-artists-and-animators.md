* Thanks to its ability to generalize, AI can turn 2D images into 3D images by getting trained on a database associating 2D with 3D images and extrapolating what it learns to new 2D images. Researchers have used such a system to create 3D facial images from selfies.[^1] While not perfect, it shows how designers that render 3D images by hand may either face near-term automation or, at the very least, have a tool in their arsenal to hasten the 3D rendering process.

* AI isn't limited to transforming existing images. After being trained on large datasets of images, it can render original but similar pictures to what it saw. This can be tremendously useful for rendering specific types of visuals at scale. For example, researchers trained an AI on street view map images, and it was subsequently able to generate realistic street scenes by itself. "One application is a new rendering pipeline for video games and animations," said a researcher on the project. "We do not need artists to create the virtual scenes manually. An AI painter can automatically learn from real images and translate the real world content to the virtual world in video games and movies. This approach can save a lot of human labor and potentially synthesize photo-realistic images."[^2]

* What about animation? Well, [Animjoy](https://www.animjoy.com) is an AI-powered tool that converts text you write into animated videos. To be honest, I wasn’t that impressed when I first tried it around August 2017, but I imagine it will improve over time. If it improves enough to allow people to create polished animated videos by simply typing a story, it could prove quite disruptive—initially to the market of freelance animators in marketplaces such as Fiverr, but possibly at some point to higher-end animators for studios such as Disney’s.
