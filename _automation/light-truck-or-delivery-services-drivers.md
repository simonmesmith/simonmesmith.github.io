* As of 2015, truck driver was the most common job in 29 of the 50 US states.[^1]
* In 2016, a White House report predicted that 2.2 to 3.1 million US jobs would be threatened or significantly altered by autonomous vehicle technology, and that truck driving would be among them.[^2]
* Light deliveries (think most Amazon packages) are well on their way to being automated. While regulatory restrictions have kept advances well-hidden from those in the US, countries with more permissive drone policies are moving forward rapidly with experiments. Matternet, for example, is testing autonomous delivery drones outside the US that can refuel and reload by themselves.[^3]
* Nvidia—maker of powerful processors for AI—is partnering with Deutsche Post DHL Group, the world’s largest mail and logistics company, to deploy a test fleet of autonomous delivery trucks in 2018.[^4]
