* Writing is a fairly broad category of activities and occupations, but everywhere you look AI is automating the process.

* Writing new brand names? Researchers have already created a system that generates appealing brand names; scores them for readability, pronounceability, memorability and uniqueness; and recommends brand names accordingly that are _more appealing than names created by humans_.[^1]

* Writing persuasive ad copy? Researchers have created a system called "PersuAIDE!" that transforms a given sentence into various forms of persuading sentences. These sentences are designed to be more persuasive by leveraging tools of persuasion such as memorability and sentiment.[^2]

* Journalism? To survive the rise of digital media and expand coverage with fewer journalists, several media outlets are embracing AI and automation. For example, the UK news agency Press Association is partnering with a company called Urbs Media to use AI for generating 30,000 localized news reports every month.[^3]

* Several companies are working to automate the creation of content, including Automated Insights, Narrative Science and Articoolo.

* Neural networks can generate novel brand names by learning from existing brand names in a product category. (See, for example, my post on [creating drug brand names with neural networks](https://simonsmith.ca/generating-drug-brand-names-with-neural-networks/).) Now companies have begun to use these names. For example, one brewery named a beer “The Fine Stranger” based on work by neural network tinkerer [Janelle C. Shane](http://lewisandquark.tumblr.com/).[^4]
