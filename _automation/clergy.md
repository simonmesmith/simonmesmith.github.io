* Clergy seem highly _unlikely_ to be automated—would people take spiritual guidance from a robot?—but early signs suggest that people project spirituality upon them, and that they may therefore see greater uptake than anticipated. 
* In April 2016, news reports described how a temple near Beijing created a small robot named Xian’er in the shape of a cartoon monk that answers questions about Buddhism and daily life.[^1]
* In May 2017, a German robot called BlessU-2  was unveiled that speaks seven languages in either a male and female voice and offers traditional, companionship, encouragement and renewal blessings.[^2]
* In August 2017, a Japanese company called Nissei Eco programmed a Pepper robot to perform Buddhist funeral rites.[^3] Their rationale? Buddhists priests weren’t getting financial support from the community and had to work other jobs to make ends meet.
