* Emergency medical providers seem unlikely to be completely automated in the near future, but aspects of their job are already being partially automated.
* For example, in October 2017 drone-delivery company [Flirtey][1] launched a world-first defibrillator delivery drone pilot for cardiac arrest in Nevada.[^1] Studies report that drones can reach the scene of a cardiac emergency way faster than an ambulance—within about five minutes, which is 17 minutes faster than an ambulance. This is particularly important for cardiac arrest, as every minute a sufferer waits to get defibrillation their chance of survival decreases by about 10%.
